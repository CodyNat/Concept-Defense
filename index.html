Concept 01 · Agriculture & Market Information

Web-Based Agricultural Market Demand & Supply Information System

With price and demand prediction for farmers and local markets — Partido Area

Problem

Farmers in the Partido area often sell harvests at low prices because they lack access to updated market demand and price information, relying heavily on middlemen. At the same time, vendors and buyers struggle to find sufficient suppliers when crops become scarce — there is no centralized platform connecting farmers, traders, vendors, and the Municipal Agriculture Office.

Proposed Solution

A centralized web platform where farmers post available harvests (crop type, quantity, location, price) while vendors and buyers post demand and required quantities. The system matches supply with demand and visualizes market trends through a shared dashboard.

Crop availability posting
Market demand posting
Supply–demand matching engine
Market information dashboard
Historical data management
Predictive analytics (ML)

User Roles

  • Administrator — manages users, data, reports, settings
  • Farmer — posts harvests, views demand & price info
  • Trader — monitors supply, facilitates transactions
  • Market Vendor / Buyer — posts requirements, searches suppliers

Tech Stack

  • BackendLaravel (PHP)
  • DatabaseMySQL
  • FrontendHTML, CSS, JS, Bootstrap
  • PredictionLinear regression / time-series

Empathize → Define → Ideate

Empathize: Farmers don't know where demand is highest and sometimes sell below market value; vendors struggle to locate suppliers; agricultural offices lack centralized monitoring data.

Define: How can a web-based agricultural information system improve communication between farmers and markets by providing centralized supply–demand information and predictive analytics to support better marketing decisions?

Ideate: A centralized platform with real-time supply/demand dashboard, price monitoring, crop search & filtering, geographic market info, historical records, and ML-based price/demand prediction.

Concept 02 · Educational Technology · 3D Visualization

GeoVision

A Web-Based Interactive 3D Geological Learning Laboratory and Reference Library with Progression-Based Learning Paths and Comparative Specimen Analysis for Geology Students of Partido State University

Problem

Geology students at PSU learn primarily through textbooks, PDFs, static images, and physical specimens restricted to lab schedules. Two-dimensional materials require students to mentally reconstruct the 3D characteristics of rocks and minerals — leading to misconceptions and limited identification skills. Beyond visualization, students lack tools for comparative specimen analysis, structured learning pathways, progress tracking, and assessment specifically designed for geology.

Visualization difficulty No interactive resources Limited specimen access No comparative tools No structured learning paths

Dual-Purpose Platform

GeoVision serves two integrated functions:

📚 Geological Reference Library Registered users freely browse a centralized specimen collection, view interactive 3D models (OBJ & GLB formats), and compare up to 3 rock or mineral specimens side-by-side with a detailed property comparison table.
🔬 Virtual Geological Learning Lab Instructors create classes, build prerequisite-based learning trees, administer standard and comparison-based quizzes, and monitor each student's progress through an integrated tracking system.

Key Features

360° rotatable & zoomable 3D models
Compare up to 3 specimens simultaneously
OBJ & GLB model format support
Prerequisite-based learning tree (auto-generated)
Standard & comparison-based quizzes
Student progress tracking
Admin-managed specimen repository
Class creation & management

Stakeholders

  • Geology Students — interactive 3D exploration, guided paths, anytime access
  • Instructors — customizable learning paths, assessments, progress monitoring
  • Partido State University — innovative geology edtech platform
  • Other Learners — Earth Science students, researchers, interested individuals
  • Future Researchers — reference for virtual labs & 3D edtech

Research Type & Area

  • TypeApplied Research / System Development
  • Area 1Educational Technology
  • Area 2Interactive Visualization
  • Area 3Web Application Development
  • Area 4Geological Education
  • Area 5Digital Learning Systems

Empathize → Define → Ideate

Empathize: Students struggle to visualize 3D specimen characteristics from 2D materials and must mentally imagine unseen angles. Physical specimen access is limited to lab hours. Students need comparison tools to identify differences in texture, composition, crystal structure, and formation. Instructors need structured digital tools for assessment and guided instruction.

Define: There is no interactive, accessible, and structured digital platform for geology students that combines 3D specimen visualization, comparative analysis, progression-based learning pathways, and assessment with progress tracking.

Ideate: GeoVision — a web-based geological reference library and virtual learning laboratory. Students explore and compare 3D specimens freely, or enroll in instructor-designed classes with prerequisite-gated learning trees, quizzes (including comparison-based), and tracked progress. Administrators manage the specimen repository using OBJ and GLB uploads.

Concept 04 · Cemetery Management · GIS & Relationship Visualization

Cemora

A Web and Mobile-Based Grid-Structured Cemetery Mapping and Family Relationship Visualization System for Efficient Grave Location and Record Management

Problem

Many cemeteries, especially under local government units, rely on paper documents, logbooks, and static maps for burial record-keeping. These manual systems make it difficult to locate graves, update records, and manage cemetery expansion. Visitors — particularly during crowded occasions like All Souls' Day — experience significant delays and emotional stress when trying to find specific gravesites, often relying solely on caretakers for assistance.

Manual record-keeping No interactive map Difficult grave location No family relationship view Unscalable layouts

Proposed Solution — What Makes Cemora Unique

Cemora digitizes cemetery management through a grid-based mapping structure where each plot is an interactive box with its own coordinate identifier. Three standout features differentiate it from a basic mapping system:

🗺️ Grid-Based Plot Mapping Each plot has a unique coordinate and clickable box on the digital grid. Cemetery expansions and relocations don't affect existing records — the grid adapts without data loss.
👨‍👩‍👧 Family Relationship Visualization Selecting a grave highlights related family members on the map using color-coded overlays and linking lines — showing burial groupings at a glance.
📍 Path Visualization Highlights a guided route from the cemetery entrance to the selected grave through the grid, helping visitors navigate unfamiliar layouts independently.
🗄️ Centralized Admin System Web-based platform for adding, updating, and managing burial records, assigning plots, and modifying the cemetery layout when expansion occurs.

Key Features

Coordinate-based grid cemetery map
Searchable burial records
Family relationship color-coding & linking
Path highlighting (entrance → grave)
Plot assignment & occupancy management
Cemetery layout expansion support
Mobile app for visitors
Web admin panel for administrators

Stakeholders

  • Visitors & Families — search & navigate to graves independently
  • Cemetery Administrators — digital record management & layout control
  • Local Government Units — scalable, organized cemetery data
  • Future Researchers — reference for grid-mapping & relationship visualization systems

Tech Stack

  • MobileFlutter
  • WebReact.js / Next.js
  • BackendNode.js
  • DatabaseMySQL / PostgreSQL
  • MappingLeaflet / Google Maps API (optional)
  • Version ControlGit & GitHub

Empathize → Define → Ideate

Empathize: Visitors struggle to locate graves in large cemeteries with outdated maps and no signage — the problem intensifies during All Souls' Day. Administrators find it hard to manage burial records and track plot availability manually. There is no way to visualize family burial groupings or guide visitors through the cemetery layout.

Define: There is no centralized and interactive cemetery mapping system that enables efficient grave location, scalable record management, and visualization of family relationships between burial plots.

Ideate: Cemora — a grid-structured cemetery map where each plot is a searchable, clickable node with burial data. Family connections are visualized with color-coded highlights and linking lines. A path visualization feature guides visitors from the entrance to the selected grave. Administrators manage the entire system — records, plots, and layouts — through a dedicated web panel, while visitors access everything through a mobile app.
