Concept 01 · Agriculture & Market Information

AgriMarket: A Web-Based Agricultural Market Demand and Supply Information System with Price and Demand Prediction for Farmers and Local Markets in the Partido Area

Connecting farmers, traders, vendors, and the Municipal Agriculture Office through a centralized supply–demand platform

Problem

Farmers in the Partido area often sell harvests at low prices because they lack access to updated market demand and price information, relying heavily on middlemen. At the same time, vendors and buyers struggle to find sufficient suppliers when crops become scarce — there is no centralized platform connecting farmers, traders, vendors, and the Municipal Agriculture Office.

Proposed Solution

A centralized web platform where farmers post available harvests (crop type, quantity, location, price) while vendors and buyers post demand and required quantities. The system matches supply with demand and visualizes market trends through a shared dashboard with ML-based predictive analytics.

Crop availability posting
Market demand posting
Supply–demand matching
Market information dashboard
Historical data management
Predictive analytics (ML)

User Roles

  • Administrator — manages users, data, reports
  • Farmer — posts harvests, views demand & prices
  • Trader — monitors supply, facilitates transactions
  • Vendor / Buyer — posts requirements, finds suppliers

Tech Stack

  • BackendLaravel (PHP)
  • DatabaseMySQL
  • FrontendHTML, CSS, JS, Bootstrap
  • PredictionLinear regression / time-series

Design Thinking

Empathize: Farmers sell below market value without knowing where demand is highest; vendors struggle to locate suppliers; agricultural offices lack centralized monitoring data.

Define: How can a web-based agricultural information system improve communication between farmers and markets by providing centralized supply–demand information and predictive analytics to support better marketing decisions?

Ideate: A centralized platform with real-time supply/demand dashboard, price monitoring, crop search & filtering, geographic market info, historical records, and ML-based price/demand prediction.

Concept 02 · Educational Technology · 3D Visualization

Geora: A Web-Based Interactive 3D Geological Learning Laboratory and Reference Library with Progression-Based Learning Paths and Comparative Specimen Analysis

For Geology Students of Partido State University

Problem

Geology students at PSU learn through textbooks, PDFs, static images, and physical specimens restricted to lab schedules. Two-dimensional materials require students to mentally reconstruct 3D characteristics — leading to misconceptions and limited identification skills. Students also lack tools for comparative specimen analysis, structured learning pathways, and assessment specifically designed for geology.

Visualization difficulty No interactive resources Limited specimen access No comparative tools No structured learning paths

📚 Geological Reference Library

Registered users freely browse a centralized specimen collection, view interactive 3D models (OBJ & GLB formats), and compare up to 3 rock or mineral specimens side-by-side with a detailed property comparison table.

🔬 Virtual Learning Laboratory

Instructors create classes, build prerequisite-based learning trees, administer standard and comparison-based quizzes, and monitor each student's progress through an integrated tracking system.

Interactive 3D Specimen Viewer — Demo

Geora uses Three.js to render OBJ and GLB geological models directly in the browser. Students can rotate, zoom, and inspect specimens from any angle without installing any software.

Open 3D Specimen Viewer

Opens in a new tab · Load any .OBJ or .GLB file from your device · Powered by Three.js

Key Features

360° rotatable & zoomable 3D models
Compare up to 3 specimens simultaneously
OBJ & GLB model format support
Prerequisite-based learning tree
Standard & comparison-based quizzes
Student progress tracking
Admin specimen repository
Class creation & management

Stakeholders

  • Geology Students — 3D exploration, guided paths, anytime access
  • Instructors — learning paths, assessments, progress monitoring
  • Partido State University — innovative geology edtech platform
  • Other Learners — Earth Science students, researchers
  • Future Researchers — reference for virtual labs & 3D edtech

Research Type & Area

  • TypeApplied Research / System Development
  • Area 1Educational Technology
  • Area 2Interactive Visualization

  • Area 4Geological Education
  • Area 5Digital Learning Systems

Design Thinking

Empathize: Students struggle to visualize 3D specimen characteristics from 2D materials and must mentally imagine unseen angles. Physical specimen access is limited to lab hours. Students need comparison tools to identify differences in texture, composition, crystal structure, and formation.

Define: There is no interactive, accessible, and structured digital platform for geology students that combines 3D specimen visualization, comparative analysis, progression-based learning pathways, and assessment with progress tracking.

Ideate: Geora — a web-based geological reference library and virtual learning laboratory. Students explore and compare 3D specimens freely, or enroll in instructor-designed classes with prerequisite-gated learning trees, quizzes (including comparison-based), and tracked progress.

Concept 03 · Educational Technology · Gamification & Cybersecurity

Cyber Investigator: A Simulation Game for Enhancing Cybersecurity Awareness and Cybercrime Detection Skills

Web-based platform using realistic cybercrime scenarios and gamification to teach cybersecurity investigation

Problem

Many students lack practical cybersecurity awareness and cybercrime detection skills because traditional learning methods focus mainly on theory and provide limited hands-on experience. There is also a lack of interactive tools that allow learners to safely practice investigating cyber threats and analyzing digital evidence.

Theory-heavy instruction No safe practice environment Limited hands-on experience Low cybercrime detection skills

Proposed Solution

A web-based simulation game where players act as cyber investigators — analyzing digital evidence, system logs, and suspicious activities to solve realistic cybercrime cases covering phishing, malware, ransomware, and data breaches. Gamification elements keep students engaged while instructors monitor outcomes through a centralized dashboard.

Realistic cybercrime scenarios
Digital evidence analysis
System log investigation
Points, badges & achievements
Leaderboards & rankings
Investigation report generation
Instructor scenario management
Student progress dashboard

Stakeholders

  • Students — safe, hands-on cybercrime investigation practice
  • Instructors — scenario management, progress monitoring

  • Future Researchers — reference for simulation-based security edtech

Technical Background

Design Thinking

Empathize: Students studying cybersecurity receive mostly theoretical instruction with little opportunity to practice real investigation workflows. Without hands-on experience in identifying threats, analyzing logs, and handling digital evidence, learners are underprepared for actual cybersecurity roles.

Define: Cyber Investigator is a web-based educational platform using interactive simulations and gamification to teach cybersecurity concepts and cybercrime investigation techniques in a safe learning environment.

Ideate: A web-based simulation game where students solve cybercrime cases as cyber investigators — collecting evidence, analyzing threats, and generating investigation reports. Points, badges, levels, and leaderboards drive engagement while instructors manage scenarios and review performance through a centralized dashboard.

Concept 04 · Cemetery Management · GIS & Relationship Visualization

Cemora: A Web and Mobile-Based Grid-Structured Multi-Cemetery Mapping and Family Relationship Visualization System with Data Verification

For Efficient Grave Location and Record Management

Problem

Many cemeteries under local government units rely on paper logs, handwritten maps, and caretaker assistance. Records are often incomplete, inconsistent, or unverified — missing plot numbers, incorrect locations, and outdated data. Visitors struggle to locate graves independently, especially during crowded occasions like All Souls' Day. Families with relatives in different cemeteries have no way to visualize cross-location burial connections.

Manual record-keeping Inconsistent burial data Difficult grave location No cross-cemetery family tracking No data verification system

🗺️ Grid-Based Multi-Cemetery Mapping

Each plot is a coordinate-tagged, clickable box on an interactive grid. The platform manages multiple cemeteries under one system — layouts adapt to expansions without disrupting existing records.

👨‍👩‍👧 Family Relationship Visualization

Within a cemetery, related graves are color-coded with linking lines. Across cemeteries, connections appear as linked profile cards with cemetery-switching functionality.

📍 Path Visualization

Highlights a step-by-step route through the grid from the cemetery entrance to the selected grave — letting visitors navigate independently without caretaker assistance.

✅ Data Verification Module

Records without plot information are automatically flagged as unverified. Administrators resolve these by assigning correct plots through the interactive map.

Key Features

Multi-cemetery grid mapping
Searchable burial records
In-cemetery family color-coding
Cross-cemetery profile linking
Path highlighting (entrance → grave)
Unverified record flagging
Admin verification & plot assignment
Cemetery layout expansion support
Mobile app for visitors
Web admin panel

Stakeholders

  • Visitors & Families — search, navigate, trace cross-cemetery family burials
  • Cemetery Administrators — centralized records & verification tools
  • Local Government Units — scalable, accurate, consistent burial data
  • Future Researchers — reference for grid-mapping & record verification

Tech Stack

  • MobileFlutter
  • WebReact.js / Next.js
  • BackendNode.js
  • DatabaseMySQL / PostgreSQL
  • MappingLeaflet / Google Maps API (optional)
  • Version ControlGit & GitHub

Design Thinking

Empathize: Visitors cannot locate graves without caretaker help, especially during peak occasions. Burial records are frequently incomplete due to years of manual entry. Families with relatives in different cemeteries cannot trace or visualize those connections. Administrators managing multiple cemeteries face high workloads with no centralized tool.

Define: There is no centralized, interactive, and scalable cemetery management system that supports accurate grave location, efficient multi-cemetery record management, cross-cemetery family relationship tracking, and data verification for incomplete or inconsistent burial records.

Ideate: Cemora — a grid-based multi-cemetery platform where each plot is a searchable, coordinate-tagged node. Family connections appear within a cemetery via color-coded links and across cemeteries via linked profiles. Path visualization guides visitors from entrance to grave. A verification module flags unverified records for admin correction, all managed from one web admin panel with a companion mobile app.
