Concept 01 · Agriculture & Market Information System

AgriMarket: A Web-Based Agricultural Market Demand and Supply Information System with Price and Demand Prediction for Farmers and Local Markets in the Partido Area

Applied Research · System Development

Problem Setting

Farmers in the Partido area often sell their harvests at low prices because they lack access to updated market demand and price information, forcing them to rely heavily on middlemen who control price negotiations. At the same time, vendors and buyers struggle to find sufficient suppliers when crops become scarce due to the absence of a centralized platform connecting all market participants.

There is no existing system that links farmers, traders, vendors, and the Municipal Agriculture Office in a shared information environment. This disconnect results in price inefficiencies, supply shortages, and missed market opportunities on both sides of the agricultural value chain.

No centralized market data Farmer dependency on middlemen No supply–demand visibility No price prediction tools Disconnected stakeholders

Project Concept

AgriMarket is a web-based agricultural market information system designed to connect farmers, traders, vendors, buyers, and the Municipal Agriculture Office through a centralized digital platform. The system enables farmers to post available harvests with crop type, quantity, location, and price, while vendors and buyers can post their demand and required quantities.

The platform provides a shared market information dashboard that visualizes current supply and demand trends, price movements, and geographic market data. By bringing all agricultural market participants into a single platform, AgriMarket aims to improve price transparency, reduce dependence on middlemen, and support informed decision-making for all stakeholders in the Partido agricultural sector.

Proposed Solution & Technical Background

AgriMarket will be developed as a web application using Laravel (PHP) for the backend and MySQL for database management, with the frontend built using HTML, CSS, JavaScript, and Bootstrap. The system will implement a supply–demand matching engine that pairs available crop listings with buyer requirements in real time.

A predictive analytics module using linear regression and time-series analysis will generate price and demand forecasts based on historical market data, helping farmers plan harvest timing and help buyers anticipate supply availability. The system will also maintain historical records of transactions and market trends for reporting and analysis by the Municipal Agriculture Office.

Empathize · Define · Ideate

Empathize: Farmers in the Partido area sell below market value without knowing where demand is highest. They rely on middlemen who take advantage of the information gap. Vendors and traders struggle to locate sufficient suppliers during peak demand periods. The Municipal Agriculture Office lacks centralized data to monitor and support the local agricultural market effectively.

Define: How can a web-based agricultural market information system improve communication and market transparency between farmers, traders, vendors, and agricultural authorities by providing centralized supply–demand information and predictive analytics to support better marketing and pricing decisions in the Partido area?

Ideate: AgriMarket — a centralized web platform with real-time supply and demand posting, a market information dashboard, price monitoring, crop search and filtering, geographic market information, historical records, and ML-based price and demand prediction. All four stakeholder roles interact through a single unified system managed by a designated administrator.

Concept 02 · Educational Technology · 3D Visualization · Geological Education

Geora: A Web-Based Interactive 3D Geological Learning Laboratory and Reference Library with Progression-Based Learning Paths and Comparative Specimen Analysis for Geology Students of Partido State University

Applied Research · System Development

Problem Setting

Geology education at Partido State University relies primarily on textbooks, PDF modules, classroom lectures, static images, and physical rock and mineral specimens available only during laboratory sessions. Most instructional materials present geological specimens in two-dimensional formats, requiring students to mentally imagine the 3D appearance, texture, crystal structure, and physical characteristics of rocks and minerals from unseen angles.

Access to physical specimens is restricted to laboratory schedules, limiting independent study. Although various online resources exist, most lack interactive three-dimensional visualization, comparative analysis tools, structured learning pathways, and assessment mechanisms specifically designed for geology education.

Visualization difficulty No interactive resources Limited specimen access No comparative analysis tools No structured learning paths No integrated assessment

Project Concept

Geora is a web-based interactive geological learning platform that serves two primary functions: a Geological Reference Library and a Virtual Geological Learning Laboratory.

As a Geological Reference Library, Geora allows registered users to freely explore a centralized collection of geological specimens, access geological information, view interactive 3D models, and compare up to three rock or mineral specimens simultaneously. The platform supports both OBJ and GLB 3D model formats, allowing administrators to continuously expand the repository.

As a Virtual Geological Learning Laboratory, Geora enables instructors to create classes, manage learning activities, design structured learning pathways using a prerequisite-based learning tree, administer assessments, and monitor student progress. Students enrolled in classes progress through learning paths by completing quizzes, including both standard and comparison-based assessments.

Proposed Solution & Technical Background

Geora will be developed as a web application integrating Three.js for 3D model rendering directly in the browser. The system supports OBJ and GLB geological model formats, allowing 360° rotation, zooming, and multi-angle inspection without additional software. A side-by-side comparison interface lets users examine up to three specimens simultaneously with a detailed property comparison table.

The learning laboratory component uses a prerequisite-based learning tree where instructors define parent-child relationships between topics and the system automatically generates the visual learning structure. Student progress, completed activities, and assessment results are tracked through an integrated monitoring system accessible to instructors.

Concept 03 · Educational Technology · Gamification · Cybersecurity

Cyber Investigator: A Simulation Game for Enhancing Cybersecurity Awareness and Cybercrime Detection Skills

Applied Research · System Development

Problem Setting

Many students lack practical cybersecurity awareness and cybercrime detection skills because traditional learning methods focus mainly on theory and provide limited hands-on experience. Classroom instruction on cybersecurity typically covers concepts, definitions, and principles without giving students the opportunity to apply these in realistic investigation scenarios.

Additionally, there is a lack of interactive tools that allow learners to safely practice investigating cyber threats, analyzing digital evidence, and responding to cybersecurity incidents. As a result, students may be underprepared to handle real-world cybersecurity challenges upon entering the workforce.

Theory-heavy instruction No safe practice environment Limited hands-on experience Low cybercrime detection skills No structured investigation practice

Project Concept

Cyber Investigator is a web-based educational simulation game that teaches cybersecurity awareness and cybercrime investigation through realistic, interactive scenarios. Players take on the role of cyber investigators, analyzing digital evidence, examining system logs, and identifying suspicious activities to solve cybercrime cases involving phishing, malware, ransomware, and data breaches.

The platform uses gamification features such as points, badges, achievements, and leaderboards to maintain student engagement and motivation throughout the learning experience. Instructors can manage scenarios, monitor individual and class-wide student progress, review investigation reports, and evaluate learning outcomes through a centralized management dashboard.

Proposed Solution & Technical Background

Cybersecurity encompasses the protection of computer systems and data from threats such as phishing, malware, ransomware, and unauthorized access. Simulation-based learning provides a controlled environment where students can safely practice cybercrime investigation without the risks associated with real systems. Combined with gamification elements — points, badges, and progression levels — simulation learning improves engagement, critical thinking, and cybercrime detection competency.

The proposed Cyber Investigator system follows an integrated architecture combining cybersecurity concepts, digital forensic investigation techniques, cybercrime detection workflows, and gamification technologies. Players interact with simulated cyber incidents, collect digital evidence, analyze suspicious activities, identify threat types, and generate investigation reports. Scores and feedback are provided based on performance to assess learning outcomes and cybersecurity competency development.

Concept 04 · Cemetery Management · GIS · Relationship Visualization

Cemora: A Web and Mobile-Based Grid-Structured Multi-Cemetery Mapping and Family Relationship Visualization System with Data Verification for Efficient Grave Location and Record Management

Applied Research · System Development

Problem Setting

Cemeteries serve as important spaces for honoring the deceased and preserving burial records for families and local communities. However, many cemeteries managed by local government units still rely on manual record-keeping systems such as paper logs, handwritten maps, and verbal assistance from caretakers. These traditional methods often lead to inefficiencies in locating graves, updating burial records, and managing cemetery expansion.

Cemetery records frequently contain inconsistencies such as missing plot numbers, incorrect locations, and unverified burial information due to years of manual data entry. Existing systems also do not support cross-cemetery family relationship tracking, limiting the ability to visualize complete family burial connections across multiple locations. During peak visitation periods such as All Souls' Day, these issues cause significant delays and emotional stress for families.

Manual record-keeping Inconsistent burial data Difficult grave location No cross-cemetery family tracking No data verification system No scalable layout management

Project Concept

Cemora is a web and mobile-based multi-cemetery mapping and information management system designed to digitize burial records and improve gravesite location services. The system uses a grid-based mapping structure where each cemetery plot is represented as an interactive, clickable box within a digital layout, with each plot assigned a unique coordinate-based identifier.

The system introduces three key visualization features: (1) family relationship visualization using color-coded highlights and linking lines for graves within the same cemetery, (2) cross-cemetery linkage through linked profile cards and cemetery-switching functionality for relatives buried in different locations, and (3) path visualization that highlights a guided route from the cemetery entrance to the selected grave through the grid.

Additionally, Cemora includes a data verification module that automatically flags records without plot information as unverified, allowing administrators to manually assign the correct plot location through the interactive map interface — ensuring long-term record accuracy and consistency.

Proposed Solution & Technical Background

Cemora integrates web technologies, mobile application development, database systems, and grid-based mapping logic to create a scalable cemetery management platform. The system consists of two main components: a web-based administrative system for cemetery personnel and a mobile application for visitors and families.

The administrative system allows authorized personnel to manage burial records, assign plots, update cemetery layouts, and handle multiple cemeteries within a single platform. The verification module enables administrators to review and correct incomplete or inaccurate records through the interactive grid map. A centralized database stores all burial records, plot coordinates, family relationship data, and user information — ensuring consistency, scalability, and easy maintenance across multiple cemetery locations.

Empathize · Define · Ideate

Empathize: Visitors cannot locate graves independently without caretaker assistance, especially during crowded occasions like All Souls' Day. Burial records are frequently incomplete or inconsistent due to years of manual data entry. Families with relatives buried across different cemeteries have no system to visualize or trace those connections. Administrators managing multiple cemeteries face high workloads with no centralized tool, making record accuracy and layout management increasingly difficult over time.

Define: The primary problem is the lack of a centralized, interactive, and scalable cemetery management system that supports efficient grave location, accurate multi-cemetery record management, cross-cemetery family relationship tracking, and data verification for incomplete or inconsistent burial records.

Ideate: Cemora — a grid-based multi-cemetery platform where each plot is a searchable, coordinate-tagged, clickable node with complete burial data. Family connections are visualized within a cemetery via color-coded highlights and linking lines, and across cemeteries via linked profile cards with cemetery-switching functionality. Path visualization guides visitors from the entrance to the selected grave. A verification module automatically flags incomplete records for administrator correction through the interactive map. All cemeteries are managed from a single web admin panel, with a companion mobile app for visitors.
