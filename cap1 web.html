Concept 01 · Agriculture & Market Information

Web-Based Agricultural Market Demand & Supply Information System

With price and demand prediction for farmers and local markets — Partido Area

Problem

Farmers in the Partido area often sell harvests at low prices because they lack access to updated market demand and price information, relying heavily on middlemen. At the same time, vendors and buyers struggle to find sufficient suppliers when crops become scarce — there is no centralized platform connecting farmers, traders, vendors, and the Municipal Agriculture Office.

Proposed Solution

A centralized web platform where farmers post available harvests (crop type, quantity, location, price) while vendors and buyers post demand and required quantities. The system matches supply with demand and visualizes market trends through a shared dashboard.

Crop availability posting
Market demand posting
Supply–demand matching engine
Market information dashboard
Historical data management
Predictive analytics (ML)

User Roles

  • Administrator — manages users, data, reports, settings
  • Farmer — posts harvests, views demand & price info
  • Trader — monitors supply, facilitates transactions
  • Market Vendor / Buyer — posts requirements, searches suppliers

Tech Stack

  • BackendLaravel (PHP)
  • DatabaseMySQL
  • FrontendHTML, CSS, JS, Bootstrap
  • PredictionLinear regression / time-series

Empathize → Define → Ideate

Empathize: Farmers don't know where demand is highest and sometimes sell below market value; vendors struggle to locate suppliers; agricultural offices lack centralized monitoring data.

Define: How can a web-based agricultural information system improve communication between farmers and markets by providing centralized supply–demand information and predictive analytics to support better marketing decisions?

Ideate: A centralized platform with real-time supply/demand dashboard, price monitoring, crop search & filtering, geographic market info, historical records, and ML-based price/demand prediction.

Concept 02 · Educational Technology · 3D Visualization

GeoVision

A web and mobile-based interactive 3D geological rock & mineral learning platform for Geology students of Partido State University

Problem

Geology students at PSU primarily learn through textbooks, PDFs, static images, and physical specimens limited to lab sessions. Two-dimensional materials make it hard to visualize the three-dimensional structure of rocks and minerals, and limited access to specimens outside lab hours restricts independent study.

Proposed Solution

An interactive learning platform offering 3D models of rocks and minerals that students can rotate, zoom, and examine from any angle, paired with educational labels covering classification, properties, composition, and formation processes. A mobile app allows offline downloads of modules and 3D specimens for areas with limited connectivity.

360° 3D specimen viewer
Educational annotations & labels
Content management (web)
Learning modules & quizzes
Offline download mode
Search & filtering tools

Stakeholders

  • Geology Students — explore 3D specimens anytime
  • Instructors — dynamic, interactive teaching tools
  • Partido State University — innovation in geology education
  • Future Researchers — reference for edtech R&D

Tech Stack

  • MobileFlutter
  • WebReact.js / Next.js
  • BackendNode.js
  • DatabasePostgreSQL / MySQL
  • 3D AssetsBlender, Photogrammetry, Meshy AI

Empathize → Define → Ideate

Empathize: Students must mentally imagine specimens from unseen angles using static images; access to physical specimens is limited to lab sessions; instructors lack interactive digital teaching tools.

Define: Lack of an interactive, accessible learning platform that lets geology students visualize, explore, and understand rocks and minerals beyond traditional classroom resources.

Ideate: GeoVision — a web & mobile platform with interactive 3D models, annotations, quizzes, downloadable offline content, and mobile accessibility to bridge traditional and digital geology instruction.

Concept 04 · GIS · Public Service Information System

EternalMap

A web and mobile-based cemetery mapping & grave information management system for LGUs and cemetery administrators

Problem

Many cemeteries in the Partido district rely on manual record-keeping — paper logbooks and physical maps — making it hard to manage burial records, track plot occupancy, and locate gravesites. Visitors often struggle to find graves, especially during occasions like All Souls' Day, due to outdated maps and lack of signage.

Proposed Solution

An interactive digital cemetery map letting visitors search for deceased individuals and view exact plot locations, with a mobile app for navigation. Administrators get a web platform to manage burial records, register new burials, update plot info, monitor occupancy, and generate reports — built on GIS mapping technologies.

Interactive digital cemetery map
Gravesite search
Navigation assistance
Burial records management
Plot occupancy monitoring
Admin reporting tools

Stakeholders

  • Visitors & Families — locate graves quickly via map & search
  • Cemetery Administrators — streamlined record & plot management
  • Local Government Units — organized burial records, better governance
  • Future Researchers — reference for GIS-based public records systems

Tech Stack

  • MobileFlutter
  • WebReact.js / Next.js
  • BackendNode.js
  • DatabasePostgreSQL / MySQL
  • MappingGoogle Maps API / Leaflet

Empathize → Define → Ideate

Empathize: Visitors waste time searching for gravesites in large cemeteries with poor signage; administrators struggle to maintain accurate records and track available plots manually.

Define: Lack of a centralized, interactive cemetery management system for efficient burial record management and accurate gravesite location services.

Ideate: EternalMap — an interactive map for searching deceased individuals and locating plots, paired with admin tools for record management, occupancy monitoring, and report generation, integrating GIS and mobile accessibility.
